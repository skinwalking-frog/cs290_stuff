A website where I list Sci-Fi movies, TV shows, Books, and Games that I have seen, read, or played and rate them based on my personal preferences. If I deem a piece of Sci-Fi media that I have consumed "bad" or "not worth mentioning" it will be omitted from this list.

Movies:

  • Blade Runner

    Score: 8

    A timeless cult classic adapted from Philip K. Dick's book "Do Andriods Dream of Electric Sheep," that everyone should see at least once. In a future where artificial humans called "replicants" are used as a disposable workforce, Deckard, a Blade Runner tasked with hunting down replicants on earth where they are illegal, must come to terms with the morality of his work and begins to question his own existence. The movie has many deeper underlying philosophical themes relating to human identity, morality, existentialism and many more. While the plot itself may not be the most coherent or even exciting, the strong use of metaphor and symbolism makes the movie stand out as a true work of science fiction.
